Missing You by Harlen Coben

I really like Harlen Cobens writing and have read 4-5 of his books. On of the things I find with thrillers is that they can stray into very dark territory, very quickly. The one before last Jack Reacher novel did the same and in both cases the content clashed with the overall tone.

That’s not to say I didn’t enjoy this one (which was the case with the Jack Reacher novel), but it was a surprise nevertheless. I also thought that in this one he relied more heavily on caricatures than in other novels and this came across as clumsy and broke the spell. The bar scenes in particular were awful and felt hugely contrived, they didn’t gel well with the other writing in the book, thankfully there wasn’t a lot of the bar in the book. Although, why it was there at all is something I would question.

Maybe, I just need to toughen up. Maybe not. I’ll read more of Harlem’s books and this is an exception to his books.
