Jack Reacher 22: Midnight Line by Lee Child

Somehow, there have been 22 Jack Reacher novels written. Somehow, I think I’ve read all of them. I don’t always like them and Jack Reacher 20 was just too dark for me. I couldn’t get past how grim the plot line was.

In both these last books Lee Childs has taken a big idea and used that as the central device in the novel. In ‘Make Me’ it was the Dark Web and in this one it was opiates.

This has been a bid deal in the US and a recent Guardian article went into a lot of detail concerning this. The plot was good and I enjoyed it. It didn’t take long to read and it had a good resolution, which is what I’m looking for in a Jack Reacher novel: bad people are doing things (probably to good people), and Jack head butts and dances (he seems to do a lot of dancing when he’s fighting) his way through them, delivering vigilante justice along the way. Perfect!

This was did that well and was a welcome return to form.
