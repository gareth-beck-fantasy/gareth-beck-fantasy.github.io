Jack Reacher 23 - Past Tense by Lee Childs

It’s becoming a tradition that I ask for the new Reacher novel as my secret Santa gift. Then buy it if I don’t get it, which is what happened this year.

Past tense isn’t a dark Reacher novel and was better for it.

Reacher novels, like Bond novels, are at their best when there’s enough reality to be believable but enough that isn’t to be enjoyable. Past Tense has that balance.

Whether the US is big enough for something like this to happen is open to debate but the characters are good and the plot thumps along at the normal Reacher pace. Writing books like this feels easy, but I suspect really isn’t.
