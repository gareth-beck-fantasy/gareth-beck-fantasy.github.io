The Glass Magician by Charlie N. Homberg

I’d managed to get in a book loop before Christmas, I’d started a book, then another, then another and, thankfully, finishing this one brought me out of that cycle. Thankfully. It was like a book inception.

So having started this and then put it down, I wasn’t sure whether I’d actually finish it. As it happened I blasted through it and really enjoyed it.

This is the second novel in the series (The Paper Magician being the first one) and they do feel like a cross between George Heyer and Harry Potter.

I loved what magic was possible with glass and that felt more interesting than paper for some reason.

There are some great twists in the book but on reflection I’m not sure that the final one stacks up.

[spoiler]

She manages to unbind from paper, then rebinds to glass. Has no-one tried that before? Seems unlikely that everyone has just followed the rules and not tested this. Maybe people are grateful for magic and the risks of losing it too high (but even then you’d probably get someone else to try).

[end spoiler]

There’s another one after this and, at some point, I’ll be reading that one.
