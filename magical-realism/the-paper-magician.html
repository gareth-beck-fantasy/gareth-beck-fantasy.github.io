The Paper Magician by Charlie N. Homberg

I’d seen this book a fair bit before I bought it and I’m glad I did. I’d always liked the cover and it’s a pretty book. The cover does a great job of distilling the books setting, which to me felt like Georgette Heyer with magic. The time period is slightly later than that, it felt Victorian but it had the same attention to detail when it came to things like clothes.

The book follows Ceony Twill as she graduates and then, rather against her wishes, is apprenticed to a paper magician. Magicians are bonded to a material and paper has declined to the point that there are only 13 practitioners left in the UK (although there is no context as to how many there are in some of the other materials).

There are some great ideas in the book and it’s a good read. The scope, and the cast, both feel small but that gives the book an intimacy, rather than being a negative.

Ceony Twill is a fantastic central character: she’s intelligent, extremely competent and, more importantly, very hard working (none of that ‘natural ability trumps hard work’ bullshit here).

If you liked books like ‘Jonathan Strange and Mr Norrell’ and ‘The Night Circus’ then this is well worth adding to your reading list.
