The End of Mr Y by Scarlett Thomas

There was something weird happening when I bought this book, as very shortly afterwards I found three more of Scarlett’s books in a second hand fair while on holiday. The name seemed familiar and it was only when I returned home that I realised that one of the books under one of the legs on my sons cot was by the same author. A book I’d had for ages, but not read, after seeing it recommended on Twitter as a very good creative writing book.

“Mr Y” is a very good read and I thought that the central idea reflected some of the work of Jean Baudrillard and Jorge Borges in that it plays with our understanding and thoughts around reality.

The central idea, namely that reality isn’t what we think it is, is hugely interesting and has been visited in films like The Matrix, Naked Lunch, High Society, They Live, to name a few. What makes Mr Y interesting is the strength of the characters, who are flawed and great, and the research that has gone into this.

It could have been a heavy read as the source material isn’t easy to get through, but it wasn’t and the material served as a backdrop for the narrative.

I for one will be doing more ‘thought experiments’ as a result.

Now to read the other three. And the creative writing book. Maybe.
