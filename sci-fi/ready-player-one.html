Ready player one by Ernest Kline

A book that cites ’80s culture and has a Ghostbusters quote in the first two pages

“Cats and dogs. Living together. Total chaos”

Was always going to tick a lot of boxes for me. I grew up during the ’80s so the references were hugely nostalgic for me.

The book centres around ‘the OASIS’, a second life style virtual reality that most of mankind has retreated into as the planet, and society are utterly borked, and more importantly the game that the creator set-up to decide the inheritor.

There are some nice touches around corporate greed and whether things like this should be monetised and those sections, for me, were thought provoking in terms of where the Internet is today.

It didn’t take long to read and I looked forward to picking it up, as I was beginning to question how much more of the format I could read it came nicely to an end, which was good.

The references come thick and fast are the manga section, rather than leave me cold, had enough detail to still be readable. I think there’s a deeper level that the book can be read on (where you geek out on the material, which I did for a lot of it) but don’t be put of thinking that you won’t get it.

I for one will be looking at iPhone ports of some of the games so loving mentioned in the book.
