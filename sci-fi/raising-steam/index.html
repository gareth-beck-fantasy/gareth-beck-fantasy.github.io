Raising Steam by Terry Pratchett

This is the third Moist Von Lipwig novel (Going Postal and Making Money) being the first two and its great. Not as good as the earlier ones but Terry has a high bar.

I stopped reading these around the time of Small Gods and have recently dug back into them.

The character reminds me of Harry Harrison’s Stainless Steel Rat: both are essentially good guys that don’t like playing by the rules, and as rules are often immoral or put in place by bad’uns it’s a great idea. I’d suggest they’re both Chaotic Good

Chaotic good characters are strong individualists marked by a streak of kindness and benevolence. They believe in all the virtues of goodness and right, but they have little use for laws and regulations.

Moist is a wonderful, playful character to read about and this whole series is an absolute joy. Well worth your time.

In the foreword Terry P mentions the watercress line, and the description of the trains (and specifically train fever) are spot on. Certainly the descriptions reminded me of a late friends love of trains. He didn’t read fiction but I would have loved to have suggested this to him.

Feel free to share!
