Dune by Frank Herbert

There’s a good reason why this regularly turns up on best of lists. It was no.39 on the BBC top 100 reads and a book that I’ve read several times.

The depth of the world building is immersive and very detailed. I didn’t find myself questioning why something was like that and nothing jarred.

It takes a young boy (15 at the start of the novel) from the lush safe world he’d grow up on and deposits him on the unforgiving world of Dune. 1

It’s been made into a mostly successful film and a TV show but none of them come close to the book.

There’s also a good/bad dance track using quotes from the film.

  1. I’m pretty sure there’s an analogy for growing up in the choice of worlds but a quick Google shows more on the link between spice and drugs and some stuff about a Christ allegory. Which I must have missed.)
