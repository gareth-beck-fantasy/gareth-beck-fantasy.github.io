Half a King series by Joe Abercrombie

I don’t know what it is about JA’s style that makes it hard for me to start but, as with Heroes, I struggled to get going.

The second book was on my shelf for so long that the third came out before I started it. As it turns out this was a good thing as I read them back to back.

Also, as with Heroes, when I did get going I loved it. JA’s work seems to be defined by characters making complex decisions. Combined with good plotting it makes for a very enjoyable read.

I was looking at the number of pages left when I hit the last third with some apprehension as there seemed a lot to wrap up in a short space of time. While the aeries wrapped up nicely, and avoided most of the happy endings that would have seemed twee, it did feel fast.

The story is told from the viewpoint of several main characters and those characters change in each book. As a device I liked it and it gave the story a feel that it was bigger than the characters in it, even as they tried to make huge changes.

I enjoyed all three books immensely but the first and second more so.

All in all, these are a very nice stand alone addition to JA’s work.
